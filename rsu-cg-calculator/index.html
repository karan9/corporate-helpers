Calculation assumptions and checks

Important before transferring figures to the ITR.

How INR values are computed
The app converts Total Proceeds using the sale TT rate. For an RSU lot, it first looks for E*TRADE's Ordinary Income Recognized Per Share and calculates quantity × that value. If unavailable, it falls back to Adjusted Cost Basis Per Share, then the total Adjusted Cost Basis, Ordinary Income Recognized, or Vest Date FMV. The resulting USD vesting cost is converted using the vesting/perquisite TT rate. A per-lot INR cost override remains available where payroll or Form 12BA provides a more authoritative vest-wise INR value.
What is not calculated
The app calculates the INR cost basis from the imported USD vesting cost and your vesting TT rate, but it does not independently calculate or reconcile salary perquisite tax. Schedule FA A2/A3, dividends, foreign-tax-credit documentation, Form 67, advance-tax interest, marginal relief, and carry-forward eligibility are outside this focused version.
Expected E*TRADE columns
The parser looks for Date Sold, Date Acquired or Vest Date, Quantity, Total Proceeds, Ordinary Income Recognized Per Share, Adjusted Cost Basis Per Share, Adjusted Cost Basis, Adjusted Gain/Loss, Symbol, Grant Number and Order Number. It skips the Summary row and consolidates repeated files by a transaction fingerprint. E*TRADE sometimes formats Vest Date FMV as an Excel date, so the app prefers the ordinary-income and adjusted-cost-basis fields.
